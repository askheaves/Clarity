1) What Is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture is a traditional Chinese practice of inserting fine, sterile needles in various points of the body to stimulate one's natural ability of healing oneself. Other adjective methods such as Electric Stimulation, Moxibustion, Cupping, Gua Sha, Chinese Herbs, and Dietary Therapy are commonly used in conjecture with needles to further strengthen the treatment.

2) How Does Acupuncture Work?

Acupuncture is based on the concept of Qi and the ability to actively control the Qi's movement. The word Qi relates to the body's energy and the effect of which on the health and physiological functions of a person. Qi flows through 14 different meridians in the body, guiding blood and fluids to nourish organs and structures along the pathways. When the body is in good health, Qi is naturally in balance. When a symptom or ailment arises, there is an imbalance in the Qi of the body. By stimulating special points, acupuncture is used to restore regular energy flow and thereby guides one's bodt to address particular problem areas.

3) What Certifications Do East Asian Medicine Practitioners Need?

In order to be licensed in the state of Washington, an acupuncturist needs to graduate from an East Asian medicine school as well as pass the National Board exam provided by the NCCAOM (National Certification Commission for Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine).

4) Why Do I Need East Asian Medicine?

Acupuncture and herbs can be used as an alternative or a conjunctive modality for conventional Western Medicine. Many diseases such as depression, insomnia, ADD/ADHD, fibromyalgia, high blood pressure, allergies and so on can be alleviated by East Asian Medicine. It is a great option for those who are interested in a natural way of medicine.
